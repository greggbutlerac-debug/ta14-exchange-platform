RECOMMENDED FIRST EXPERIENCE

1. Open the Governance Workspace.

2. Open the Visual Route Builder.

3. Construct or load an AI Governance route.

4. Complete the eight declared route stages.

5. Transfer the route into the evaluation workspace.

6. Confirm that the route remains identified as AI Governance.

7. Review the AI Governance adapter result.

8. Observe that declared stages do not automatically produce ALLOW.

9. Review each requirement marked SATISFIED, UNKNOWN, or UNSATISFIED.

10. Confirm that unresolved evidence, authority, execution, or outcome proof produces HOLD.


VENDOR-PAYMENT DEMONSTRATION

The original executable demonstration remains available.

It represents a vendor payment above USD 25,000.

The initial route is designed to produce HOLD because required dual authority and beneficiary proof are missing.

The route may then be corrected and rerun.

The original HOLD must remain preserved.

Correction does not erase the adverse decision that came before it.


AI GOVERNANCE EVALUATION

The Visual Route Builder can transfer an AI Governance route into the evaluation workspace.

The AI Governance route is not submitted to the vendor-payment API.

The platform does not invent:

- Supplier identity
- Invoice identity
- Beneficiary identity
- Payment value
- Authority proof
- Evidence objects
- Execution receipts
- Outcome receipts

The AI Governance adapter evaluates the route within its own domain boundary.

A complete textual route may still receive HOLD when required proof remains UNKNOWN.

IMPORTANT DEVELOPMENT BOUNDARY

V0.3.2 is a working development and demonstration platform.

It is not yet a complete production custody, identity, payment, certification, or independent-verification system.

Do not submit production secrets, protected health information, financial credentials, regulated evidence, access tokens, private customer records, or signing keys to a development deployment.


CURRENT LIMITATIONS

The current release does not yet provide:

- Complete production authentication
- Final tenant isolation
- Production private evidence custody
- Managed hardware-backed signing keys
- Final key rotation and revocation
- Production Stripe entitlement processing
- Independently operated certification
- Independently operated verification authority
- Final enterprise access controls
- Final disaster-recovery controls
- Production legal-hold controls
- Complete production audit separation

Selected execution, bypass, replay, duplicate-payment, and outcome observations remain visibly bounded demonstration fixtures.


PERSISTENCE BOUNDARY

Local development records and development storage must be treated as demonstration records.

A visible route record is not automatically a production custody record.

Production deployment requires:

- Authenticated access
- Tenant separation
- Durable database persistence
- Private evidence storage
- Backup and recovery
- Retention controls
- Legal-hold controls
- Access logging
- Administrative separation
- Incident response
- Disaster recovery


SIGNING BOUNDARY

A valid digital signature proves that an artifact corresponds to a signing key and that the signed content has not changed.

It does not automatically prove:

- That the underlying evidence was true
- That the signer possessed lawful authority
- That the execution occurred
- That the outcome was accurate
- That the system was uncompromised
- That an independent authority reviewed the route

Development keys must not be used as production trust anchors.


FREE TESTING AND DURABLE PRESERVATION

Users may construct and test routes without paying for a favorable decision.

The commercial preservation boundary is separate from the decision.

Payment does not purchase ALLOW.

A preserved route may remain:

- ALLOW
- HOLD
- DENY
- ESCALATE

Adverse decisions must remain preserved exactly as issued.
